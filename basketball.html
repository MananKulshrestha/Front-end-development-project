Basketball

Basketball is a fast-paced game played by two teams of five players on a rectangular court. The objective is to shoot a ball through a hoop 18 inches in diameter and 10 feet high mounted to a backboard at each end.

Basic Gameplay

Teams score by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop. A regular field goal is worth two points, while a shot made from beyond the three-point line is worth three points. Players advance the ball by bouncing it while walking or running (dribbling) or by passing it to a teammate.

Common Violations

  • Traveling: Taking more than one and a half steps without dribbling the ball.
  • Double Dribble: Dribbling the ball with both hands simultaneously or stopping your dribble and then starting again.
  • Shot Clock Violation: In professional leagues, a team has a set amount of time (usually 24 seconds) to attempt a shot that hits the rim.

Player Positions

The standard basketball court is highly structured. [Image of basketball court lines and dimensions diagram] The five players typically fill distinct roles: Point Guard (playmaker), Shooting Guard (scorer), Small Forward (versatile wing player), Power Forward (strong rebounder), and Center (the tallest player, usually playing near the basket).
