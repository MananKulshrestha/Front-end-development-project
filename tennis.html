Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport that can be played individually against a single opponent (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles). Each player uses a tennis racket to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over or around a net.

Basic Gameplay

The object of the game is to maneuver the ball in such a way that the opponent is not able to play a valid return. Matches are broken down into points, games, and sets. The unique scoring system progresses from Love (zero) to 15, 30, and 40.

Court Surfaces

Tennis is unique in that it is professionally played on several different types of surfaces, which heavily affects the speed and bounce of the ball:

  • Hard Court: Made of rigid materials. Offers a predictable bounce and medium-fast pace.
  • Clay Court: Made of crushed shale, stone, or brick. Slows down the ball and creates a high bounce.
  • Grass Court: The traditional surface. Very fast and the ball bounces low and inconsistently.

The Court Layout

A tennis court is divided by a net in the middle. It features baselines at the far ends, service boxes near the net where serves must land, and tramlines on the sides that distinguish the wider boundaries used for doubles matches.
