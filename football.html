Football (Soccer)

Football is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players who primarily use their feet to propel a ball around a rectangular field called a pitch. The objective of the game is to score more goals than the opposition by moving the ball beyond the goal line into the opposing goal.

Basic Gameplay

The team that scores the most goals by the end of the 90-minute match wins. Players are assigned specific roles: Goalkeepers defend the net, Defenders stop opposing attackers, Midfielders link the defense to the offense, and Forwards focus on scoring.

Key Rules

  • No Hands: Except for the goalkeeper within their penalty area, players cannot deliberately handle the ball with their hands or arms.
  • The Offside Rule: An attacking player cannot be closer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is played to them.
  • Fouls and Cards: Unfair physical contact or rule-breaking results in a foul. Severe offenses are penalized with Yellow (warning) or Red (ejection) cards.

The Pitch

A standard adult football pitch is rectangular. It features a halfway line, a center circle, two penalty areas (the larger boxes around the goals), and two goal areas (the smaller boxes immediately in front of the goals).
