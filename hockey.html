Hockey

Hockey is a term used to denote various types of both summer and winter team sports which originated on either an outdoor field, sheet of ice, or dry floor such as in a gymnasium. The most popular forms are Field Hockey and Ice Hockey.

Basic Gameplay

Two teams play against each other by trying to maneuver a ball or a puck into the opponent's goal using a hockey stick. It is a highly demanding physical game known for rapid transitions between offense and defense.

Field Hockey vs. Ice Hockey

  • Field Hockey: Played on turf or grass with a hard plastic ball. Sticks are J-shaped and players can only use the flat side of the stick. Teams consist of 11 players.
  • Ice Hockey: Played on an ice rink using ice skates and a flat rubber puck. It is a full-contact sport played in three 20-minute periods with 6 players per team on the ice at a time.

Key Rules (Field Hockey)

To ensure safety, players are not allowed to swing their sticks above shoulder height (high-sticking). Additionally, a goal can only be scored from inside the "striking circle," a semi-circle area directly in front of the opponent's goal.
